Different types of dining tables -

Rectangular dining tables - Their strong lines and geometric shape lend a formal quality, and in a lengthy and narrow room, a rectangular table is an ideal shape to fill the void. But this type of dining table isn’t always so vast. Rectangular tables are a popular choice in homes where they can seat as few as four, with room for two extra people at the head of the table when there are guests over.

Square dining tables - Square dining tables are the best space-saving dining tables, given how they can be tucked in against a wall and moved out of the way if need be. To truly save space, you can opt for angular, armless dining chairs or benches that will slide under to take up minimal floor space. More intimate than its rectangular counterpart, a square table is sleek and contemporary.

Circular dining tables - Circular dining tables tend to soften the atmosphere in the room. They can fit a high number of people around its circumference, given that the lack of edges doesn’t restrict the number of seats so you can put them all the way around.

Oval dining tables - Oval dining tables are a blend between rectangle and circular tables, as two sides are more linear while the ends are rounded. These are best suited for larger rooms.
